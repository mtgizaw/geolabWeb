We take on a wide range of applied and basic research projects at the intersection between artificial intelligence, machine learning, and large-scale spatial data analysis. Each of our projects focuses on one of three core questions:

Vibrancy & Diversity

How can we create and promote a vibrant society of data driven policymakers?

Projects in this area seek to empower the disempowered by increasing the diversity of those that can participate in data-driven decisionmaking. We do this by providing free access to critical "building block" datasets and technologies, and training the next generation of leaders in the use of data for decisionmaking activities.

View All of Our Vibrancy & Diversity Projects
Sustainability

Are the actions we are taking to mitigate climate change working?

Projects in this area seek to mitigate negative human implications of a changing climate, focusing on the identificaiton of solutions that have worked - or, not - to date. We do this by developing new methodologies which take advantage of freely available satellite imagery to monitor mitigation and adaptation strategies all across the globe.

View All of Our Sustainability Projects
Security

How are human livelihoods changing in some of the world's most inaccessible regions?

Projects in this area seek to reduce the likelihood that global citizens are put in harms way when operating in data-sparse, inaccessible regions. We do this by collecting and publishing event-based data on where country actors are conducting activities that may mediate security environments around the world, and deriving new machine learning approaches to remotely detect shifts in sentiment or human infrastructure of relevance to the global security environment.
