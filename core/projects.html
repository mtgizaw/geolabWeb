We take on a wide range of applied and basic research projects at the intersection between artificial intelligence, machine learning, and large-scale spatial data analysis. Each of our projects focuses on one of three core areas:

Vibrancy & Diversity

Projects in this area seek to empower the disempowered by increasing the diversity of those that can participate in data-driven decisionmaking. We do this by providing free access to critical "building block" datasets and technologies, and training the next generation of leaders in the use of data for decisionmaking activities.

Sustainability

Projects in this area seek to mitigate negative human implications of a changing climate, focusing on the identificaiton of solutions that have worked - or, not - to date. We do this by developing new methodologies which take advantage of freely available satellite imagery to monitor mitigation and adaptation strategies all across the globe.

Security

Projects in this area seek to reduce the likelihood that citizens of the United States and its allies are put in harms way. We do this by collecting and publishing event-based data on where country actors are conducting activities that may mediate security environments around the world, and deriving new machine learning approaches to detect shifts in sentiment or infrastructure of relevance to the global security environment.
